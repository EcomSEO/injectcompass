# InjectCompass — Affiliate Partner Reference

The affiliate brands InjectCompass reviews, the networks that carry them, and the application strategy.

**Hard rule (repeated for emphasis):** **No affiliate links to peptide vendors, compounded peptide pharmacies, or research-peptide suppliers. Ever.** This is the line that triggers FDA letters. It is non-negotiable, site-ending if crossed.

**Last updated:** April 2026 — check rates/availability before onboarding.

## 2. Insulin syringes & needles (Wave 1 — the core commercial hub)

**Post this supports:** "Needle Sizes for Peptide Injection" pillar, "Best Insulin Syringes for Peptide Injection," "29G vs 30G vs 31G"

| Brand | Spec | Why | Affiliate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BD Ultra-Fine Nano** | 31G × 5/16" (8mm) | The category default. Trusted brand. Most prescriptions in US are written around BD. | Amazon (~3%) + ADW Diabetes (~8%) |
| **Easy Touch** | 31G × 5/16" | Budget alternative. Decent quality. $/needle is meaningful. | Amazon |
| **Exel Comfort Point** | 31G × 5/16" | Third alternative. ADW Diabetes stocks it. | ADW Diabetes |
| **BD Ultra-Fine II** | 30G × 5/16" | For pen use (matches pen threading on some devices) | Amazon + ADW |
| **BD SafetyGlide** | 30G × 1/2" | For vial-and-syringe use when 5/16" isn't long enough | Amazon |
| **Kendall Monoject** | 31G × 5/16" | Hospital-brand, reliable | ADW Diabetes |
| **NovoFine Plus** | 32G × 5/16" | For pen users (specific to Novo Nordisk pens — Ozempic, Victoza) | Amazon |

### Brands to name-and-skip
- Generic unbranded Amazon syringes — highly variable quality, counterfeit risk
- Any syringe without Luer-lock that claims to be for peptide injection
- Acupuncture-grade needles

### Commission expectation
Syringes are low-AOV ($15-50 per pack) but high-volume. Blended 4-7% commission. Direct medical-supply affiliates outperform Amazon per click.

## 7. Newsletter sponsorship (the other revenue engine)

Per research doc §9, newsletter sponsorship at $60 CPM × 4 sends/month × 15k subs by month 12 = ~$3.6k/mo.

Target sponsor categories (no conflicts of interest):
- Medical supply companies (diabetes supplies, home health equipment)
- Travel accessory brands
- NOT: drug manufacturers (conflict with editorial independence)
- NOT: telehealth clinics (conflict + YMYL compliance issue)
- NOT: any peptide seller, research or otherwise (site-ending)

Sponsorship slot format: one per newsletter, labeled "This week's sponsor:" + 60-word disclosure + link. Visually distinct from editorial. Transparent.

## 8. What we will never affiliate-link to

The list, written plainly:

- Any peptide vendor (BPC-157 seller, TB-500 seller, research-peptide marketplace)
- Any compounded-pharmacy GLP-1 seller
- Any "peptide protocol" coaching service
- Any telehealth clinic offering prescriptions
- Any drug manufacturer's patient-portal referral
- Anabolic steroid vendors
- SARM vendors
- "Longevity supplement" vendors whose products aren't third-party tested

If a category isn't listed above but feels like it belongs: ask. Default to no.

## 9. Revenue model — month 6 + month 12 projections

Research doc §9 projections, with my notes:

### Month 6 (~60k sessions, per research roadmap)

- Affiliate: 60k × 2% CTR × 3% conv × $4 avg = ~$1.4k/mo
- Newsletter: 3k subs × one monthly sponsor at $50 CPM = ~$150/mo
- **Blended: ~$1.5-2.0k/mo**

### Month 12 (~200k sessions, per research)

- Affiliate: 200k × 2% CTR × 3% conv × $4 avg = ~$4.8k/mo (weighted toward Frio/VIVI sales)
- Newsletter: 15k subs × 4 sends × $60 CPM = ~$3.6k/mo
- **Blended: $6-9k/mo** (per research §9)

### Key leverage points
1. **The Peptide Calculator is traffic's job-done.** It doesn't monetize directly. It drives calculator-completions → newsletter signups → eventual affiliate conversions.
2. **Travel posts are the single highest-RPM post type.** $15-20 per VIVI Cap sale makes a travel-post reader worth 50x a swab-post reader.
3. **Cross-newsletter pollination with PepTips + PepVise.** A reader who subscribes to InjectCompass likely signs up for PepTips for drug-specific content and PepVise for research-context. Three touchpoints per reader amortizes CAC dramatically.
